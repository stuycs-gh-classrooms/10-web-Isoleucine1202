Resources

Campbell Biology, by Reece et al.
The primary textbook for the USABO and most other biology competitions. Spanning 56 chapters of nearly every topic necessary to know in the USABO, Campbell Biology is a must-read when participating in biolympiads.
Pandorax
Created by a past USABO National Finalist, Pandorax is an incredible resource. It contains comprehensive guides not only on biology but also calculus, earth science, astrophysics, and beyond. It can be found here.
Libgen
A relatively controversial resource, Libgen is a free textbook and scientific paper downloading site. It contains virtually any textbook or even novel one could think of, and is great for obtaining hard-to-find resources or expensive textbooks. It can be found here.
Molecular Biology of the Cell by Alberts et al.
MBOC is one of the best cell biology textbooks available. Although it tends to be highly complex and definitely goes beyond the standard curriculum for any biolympiad, it is an amazing resource if aiming for national finals and above.
Genetics Analysis and Principles by Robert J. Brooker
Brooker's Genetics is certainly one of the choice textbooks for genetics. It provides a comprehensible description of most studied topics and is wholly necessary for semifinal levels and beyond.
Lehninger Principles of Biochemistry by David L. Nelson and Micheal M. Cox
An excellent but highly advanced textbook, Lehninger Principles of Biochemistry is an excellent book for delving deeper into biochemistry and advanced cell bio. While not suggested for open or semifinal levels, Lehninger is needed to reach National Finalist and beyond.
Vander's Human Physiology by Widmaier et al.
Vander's is undeniably the best textbook for anatomy and physiology- used not only for biolympiads but also for science bowl and science olympiad contests, one cannot have studied A&P without having studied Vander's.
